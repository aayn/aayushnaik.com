**Caveats:**

- Net worths are taken from the 2026-03-10 snapshot — these match the values Forbes published in its annual list, which uses share prices and exchange rates locked on a single February 2026 date. (Real-time figures drift daily.)
- The numeric Forbes Self-Made Score (1–10) is only published for a minority of profiles in this rank tier — Forbes assigns scores comprehensively for the top of the list and selectively below. The binary self-made/inherited classification, however, is broadly applied. The 28 individuals lacking even a binary classification are predominantly Asian (Chinese, Japanese, Korean) and Middle Eastern billionaires for whom Forbes' research team did not publish a definitive ruling.
- Country reflects citizenship as recorded by Forbes; some billionaires reside elsewhere (e.g. several Chinese citizens reside in Singapore or Hong Kong; Russian-born billionaires often hold dual citizenship).
- 'Source of Wealth' uses Forbes' own short-form description, with industry classification appended in parentheses.
- Family entries (e.g. 'Rodger Riney & family') represent a single Forbes line item with shared net worth attributed to a primary holder.
