### Gaps & caveats
- The realtimebillionaires.de mirror tracks 3,408 Forbes entries; the final 17 Forbes ranks (3412–3428) are not visible in any third-party aggregator I could access. Forbes' own interactive directory is bot-blocked. These ranks all hold $1.0B and are typically the very last entries to clear the threshold.
- Note rank numbering offset: my list shows Joe Kiani at #3001 (consistent with Forbes' 3017 reported elsewhere — minor real-time drift). Ranks here approximate Forbes' list-day positions; ties are common at $1.0–1.3B.
- Self-Made Score follows Forbes' methodology (1 = inherited and didn't grow it; 10 = self-made from no advantages). Where Forbes' published score wasn't available for tail-end entries, I assigned scores based on documented background:
  - Score 1–2: heirs of intact family fortunes (Cargill MacMillans, Hagers, Voigts, Lerners, Caprottis, Kamprads, Maly Malones, Ekaterina Fedun, Beyoncé/celebrity exception aside).
  - Score 3–5: heirs who expanded inherited businesses (Burmans, Feffers, Tonnies brothers, Iervolino is self-made).
  - Score 7: self-made first-generation Chinese/Russian/Asian entrepreneurs (post-reform era — most have no documented family wealth but Forbes typically rates 7 for Chinese first-gen).
  - Score 8–10: documented founders or pure self-made (Springsteen, Rowling, Cameron, Beyoncé, Rihanna, Dr. Dre — score 10 as no inherited capital; Pandey, Mensch, Coplan, Kushner — Kushner inherited family real estate empire so 5).
- For some Chinese, Russian and other entries with limited English-language profiles, the source-of-wealth descriptions are abbreviated industry summaries.
- Family group entries listed individually (e.g., Cargill MacMillans, Voigt children) reflect Forbes' practice of listing each beneficiary separately.
- Net worth values reflect Forbes' March 1, 2026 measurement; minor drift since publication is expected.
