Research notes for the essay *"Billionaires should not exist."* All figures from the Forbes 2026 World's Billionaires List (published March 10, 2026) unless otherwise noted.

## 3. Wealth Source — Self-Made vs. Inherited

### Forbes' Self-Made Score (1–10)

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Inherited, hasn't grown it |
| 2–5 | Inherited, grew it |
| 6 | Inherited capital, expanded with hired help |
| 7 | Self-made with family support |
| 8 | Self-made from middle-class background |
| 9–10 | Self-made from no advantages |

### Headline numbers (score 6+ counts as "self-made")

| Region | "Self-made" share | Inherited share |
|--------|-------------------|------------------|
| Global | ~67% | ~33% |
| United States | 73% | 27% |
| China / Russia | 97% | 3% |
| Women globally | 25% | 75% |
| Men globally | 67% | 33% |

### Important caveat for the debate

Forbes' "self-made" definition is **permissive**. A score of 6 includes people who inherited significant capital and expanded existing businesses. By the stricter "started with no advantages" criterion (scores 9–10), the share drops dramatically — critics including Robert Reich and the United for a Fair Economy report argue that <30% of US billionaires are "self-made" by any meaningful standard. The Forbes metric counts a tech founder whose parents seeded a $300K loan as equivalent to one who started with nothing.

China and Russia's near-100% "self-made" rates reflect that their billionaire classes are largely first-generation (post-1990s privatization in Russia; post-1980s reform-era wealth in China).

In 2024, 60% of *new* billionaires were self-made (reversing the prior year's inheritor majority) — driven heavily by AI, crypto, and platform companies.

## 6. Patterns Worth Examining

1. **Pareto-of-Pareto**: Top 20 (= 0.6% of all billionaires) hold ~19% of all billionaire wealth.
2. **Geographic concentration**: 51% of billionaires (and ~58% of wealth) live in three countries.
3. **Tech dominates the top, finance dominates the count.** Different stories at different scales.
4. **"Self-made" is leaky**: Forbes' definition lets through many with significant inherited advantages. The "no advantages whatsoever" share is much smaller.
5. **Inheritance compounds across generations**: Walton, Mars, Koch, Hermès each represent multi-generational wealth that has *grown* not eroded — directly contradicting the "shirtsleeves to shirtsleeves in three generations" trope.
6. **Wealth concentration is accelerating, not stabilizing**: +$4T in one year. The class is getting larger and richer simultaneously.
7. **Platform shifts mint new billionaires**: AI (45 new in 2026), crypto, music streaming, K-pop. Each platform shift extracts new fortunes.
8. **Gender asymmetry in inheritance**: 75% of women billionaires inherited; only 33% of men did. Women's path to billions still runs primarily through marriage or family.
