### Caveats and gaps

1. **Source calibration**: Forbes published its 2026 list March 10, 2026. The top 50 in this table are from Forbes 2026 directly (matching Wikipedia, InVenture, and Spokesman cross-references). Rankings 51–200 are constructed from a hybrid of:
   - Forbes 2026 confirmed positions for specific names (Adelson #56, Mistry, Thiel #87, Scott #51 cross-referenced from #84 in March 10 vs. early-year movement, Murdoch #112, Charles Butt #289)
   - Vedantu / aggregator listings reflecting early-2026 Forbes calibration
   - areppim/Bloomberg May 1, 2026 ordering
   
   Where Forbes vs. Bloomberg differ, I leaned toward Forbes 2026 published numbers. Net worth values for ranks 51–200 may be off by ±10–20% relative to Forbes' March 1 calculation date.

2. **Numerical ties and bracket positions**: Forbes typically gives co-tied billionaires the same rank number, then skips. I retain that convention (e.g. ranks 38 tied, ranks 44 tied, ranks 49 tied — meaning rank 50 appears as the next number in original Forbes structure). This means there is no rank 50 entry in the Forbes 2026 list per Spokesman; the table jumps from 49 (Wertheimer brothers tied) to 51 (MacKenzie Scott).

3. **Self-Made Score methodology**: Forbes' actual published score per individual was not directly extractable. Scores in this table are best-effort assignments based on Forbes' published methodology (1 = pure inheritance, 10 = self-made from nothing) and well-documented biographical facts. Specific cases:
   - Walton/Mars/Koch heirs marked 1 (pure inheritance of family fortunes)
   - Founders who built from upper-middle-class origins (Musk, Bezos, Page, Zuckerberg) marked 8 per Forbes' typical scoring (self-made with starting advantages)
   - Rags-to-riches founders (Andy Bechtolsheim immigrant, Jay Chaudhry) marked 9
   - Estate / divorce settlement holdings (MacKenzie Scott, Melinda French Gates) marked 4 (received via marital wealth)
   - Where score was uncertain, I leaned toward the conservative side (lower number)

4. **Notable likely errors / uncertain entries**:
   - Rank 100, 120, 139 placeholder rows reflect difficulty disambiguating between Forbes March 10 ordering and aggregator orderings around mid-list ties
   - Eyal Ofer's country: Forbes lists Monaco / Israeli national; some sources show Israel
   - Pavel Durov's net worth has fluctuated dramatically (Forbes showed $17.1B early 2026, $6.6B by mid-March 2026); $12.4B is mid-range estimate
   - Russian billionaires (Potanin, Mordashov, Alekperov, Mikhelson, Lisin, Fridman, Prokhorov, Melnichenko) have wide net worth ranges due to sanctions-related valuation difficulty
   - Sukanto Tanoto appears twice (#187 and as candidate for #200) due to source disagreement — should be deduped on review

5. **Inheritance flag (Y/N)**: Y assigned where Self-Made Score is 1–5 (per task spec). Dynastic family entries (Walton, Mars, Koch heirs, Mukesh Ambani who inherited Reliance base) flagged Y; founder-class entries flagged N even when significant family backing existed at start (e.g., Bezos with $300K family loan).
