The compatibilist subject-change

The compatibilist move1 feels like changing the subject. If we redefine "free" to mean "uncoerced," we get a notion that fits the world but leaves the original question entirely untouched.

Strawson's regress

Strawson2 presses harder. To be truly responsible, you would have to be the cause of your own character. But your character is the product of nature and nurture, neither of which you chose.

Rules

  • TOC location   Top of right rail. position: sticky; top: 12px — stays visible as you scroll. Sidenotes flow below it; if a sidenote near the top of the page would overlap, it sits below the TOC.
  • TOC content   Auto-generated from H2 headings (and optionally H3 nested). No manual maintenance.
  • TOC active state   Highlights the current section as you scroll (intersection observer).
  • TOC conditional   Hidden if essay has fewer than 2 H2 sections — short essays don't need an outline.
  • TOC mobile   Collapsible drawer at top of essay. Single tap to expand; auto-collapses when a link is followed.
  • Backlinks location   "Linked from" section at the bottom of essay body, before footer.
  • Backlinks generation   Build-time scan: parse every essay's MDX, find links to other essays, build a reverse-lookup map. Pass to layout per essay.
  • Backlinks display   Linking essay's title (serif) + subtitle (italic) + a snippet showing the linking sentence/paragraph with the link emphasized.
  • Backlinks conditional   Section only renders if at least one backlink exists. New essays don't show an empty "Linked from."
  • Backlinks ordering   Most-recently-modified first.
